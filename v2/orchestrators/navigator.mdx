Use this page to decide whether Livepeer orchestration fits your hardware, time, and operating model before you start setup.

## 1. Should you run an orchestrator?

You are likely a fit if all of the following are true:

- You want direct control over pricing, uptime, and workload mix.
- You are comfortable operating GPU infrastructure and handling incidents.
- You can support the operational cost of a public node, not just the install.

You are likely better off joining a pool if you want GPU earnings without staking, protocol operations, or public service management.
