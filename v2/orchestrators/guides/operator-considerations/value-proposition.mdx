This page is for people who are considering running a Livepeer orchestrator but haven't committed yet. It covers the real costs, revenue ceiling questions, and practical decision points — so you can make an honest assessment before spending time on setup.

If you've already decided to run a node and just need to set it up, go directly to [Setup Paths](/v2/orchestrators/guides/setup-paths/setup-navigator).

<CustomDivider />

## What you can earn

Orchestrators earn from two sources. Understanding both matters before you begin:

<StyledTable variant="bordered">
  <TableRow header>
    <TableCell header>Revenue stream</TableCell>
    <TableCell header>How it works</TableCell>
    <TableCell header>Depends on</TableCell>
  </TableRow>
  <TableRow>
    <TableCell>**ETH job fees**</TableCell>
    <TableCell>Gateways pay you per transcoding segment or AI inference job</TableCell>
    <TableCell>Your capability, pricing, performance, uptime</TableCell>
  </TableRow>
  <TableRow>
    <TableCell>**LPT inflation rewards**</TableCell>
    <TableCell>Protocol mints LPT each round; you claim your share by calling `Reward()`</TableCell>
    <TableCell>Your total bonded stake, inflation rate, reward call reliability</TableCell>
  </TableRow>
</StyledTable>

Neither stream pays from day one without setup. ETH fees require gateways to route work to you. LPT rewards require bonded LPT, an active orchestrator registration, and a running node that calls `Reward()` each round.

For the full mechanics, see [Earnings Explained](/v2/orchestrators/guides/staking-and-rewards/earnings).

## What it costs

Before modelling any revenue, be honest about costs. These are the real categories:

<AccordionGroup>

<Accordion  title="Hardware" icon="server">

The primary upfront cost. You need at least one NVIDIA GPU.

- **Video transcoding only:** An NVIDIA GPU with NVENC/NVDEC support. Consumer RTX cards (3060, 3070, 3080) are common. GPU is recommended but transcoding without a GPU is possible at significantly lower throughput.
- **AI inference (batch):** 8–24 GB VRAM depending on which pipelines you want to run. An RTX 3090 (24 GB) is the practical minimum for competitive diffusion pipelines.
- **AI inference (real-time / Cascade):** 24 GB VRAM minimum. Real-time pipelines are more demanding than batch.

See [GPU and Hardware Reference](/v2/orchestrators/guides/feasibility-and-hardware/hardware-reference) for specific per-workload requirements.

Hardware you already own has zero ongoing cost. Hardware you're buying to run Livepeer has an amortisation cost you should model explicitly: if you spend £800 on an RTX 4090 and earn £30/month, the payback period is over 2 years before electricity.

</Accordion>

<Accordion  title="LPT stake (for video transcoding)" icon="video">

To receive video transcoding jobs as a solo orchestrator, you must be in the **active set** — currently the top orchestrators by total bonded stake.

{/* REVIEW: Verify current active set size — was top 100 per gpu-nodes-tab-audit.md and historical docs, but this may have changed. Rick to confirm. */}

LPT must be acquired and bonded to your orchestrator address. You cannot borrow it. Acquiring meaningful LPT at current market prices is the primary entry barrier for solo video orchestrators.

**If you don't have sufficient LPT for the active set, you have two alternatives:**
1. Join a pool as a worker (no LPT needed — the pool orchestrator handles on-chain operations)
2. Run AI inference only (AI job routing prioritises capability and price over stake position)

For stake requirements by path, see the decision matrix below.

</Accordion>

<Accordion  title="ETH for gas on Arbitrum" icon="link">

Your orchestrator wallet needs ETH on Arbitrum One for:

- **Reward() calls** — one per round, approximately every 22 hours. Cost: ~$0.01–0.12 per call at current Arbitrum gas prices.
- **Ticket redemptions** — periodic. Cost: ~$0.01–0.05 per redemption batch.
- **Activation transaction** — one-time setup cost.

This is a running cost but very low on Arbitrum L2. Budget roughly $5–15/month in ETH for a mid-volume node. A depleted ETH wallet means missed reward rounds (permanent loss) and unredeemed winning tickets.

</Accordion>

<Accordion  title="Bandwidth" icon="circle-question">

Video transcoding is bandwidth-intensive. Each live stream you transcode requires approximately:
- **6 Mbps download** (source stream)
- **5.6 Mbps upload** (output renditions)

{/* REVIEW: ABR ladder from v1 set-session-limits.mdx (1080p/720p/480p/360p/240p). Verify this is still the common rendition set on the current network — has the standard ladder changed? Ask Rick or check recent gateway requests in Discord. */}

A 1 Gbps symmetric connection supports approximately 100+ concurrent streams on bandwidth alone. For most home operators (100–500 Mbps), bandwidth becomes the session limit constraint before hardware does.

AI inference is much lighter on bandwidth per job — it's a single image or audio clip in, a result out, rather than a continuous stream.

</Accordion>

<Accordion  title="Time" icon="circle-question">

Orchestrator operation is not completely passive. Ongoing time costs include:

- Initial setup and configuration: 4–16 hours
- Monitoring and maintenance: 1–3 hours/week once stable
- Troubleshooting edge cases: variable
- Keeping go-livepeer updated: 30–60 minutes per release

Pool workers have minimal ongoing time investment once the worker process is configured. Solo orchestrators require more consistent attention, especially for reward calling reliability and gateway relationship management.

</Accordion>

## The three viability questions

Before choosing a path, answer these three honestly:

### 1. Is reward calling profitable for my stake?

Calling `Reward()` costs gas. If your stake is small, the LPT you'd receive per round may be worth less than the gas cost.

A rough check: open [explorer.livepeer.org](https://explorer.livepeer.org) and find your orchestrator's "Estimated Reward This Round." If that LPT value (converted to ETH at the current price) is less than approximately 0.00005 ETH ($0.12 at ETH=$2,500), the gas cost may exceed the reward. In that case, start with manual reward calling until your stake grows.

This threshold changes with LPT price, ETH price, and inflation rate. It is not a fixed number. See [Rewards and Fees](/v2/orchestrators/guides/staking-and-rewards/rewards-and-fees) for the detailed calculation.

### 2. Can I compete on pricing and capability?

For video transcoding: gateways filter orchestrators by both stake position and price. If your `pricePerUnit` is above a gateway's `maxPricePerUnit`, you receive no jobs from that gateway regardless of your hardware.

For AI inference: gateways filter first by capability (do you have the requested pipeline and model?), then by price. Stake matters less here — a well-configured AI node with warm models can compete from day one.

Check current competitive pricing at [explorer.livepeer.org/orchestrators](https://explorer.livepeer.org/orchestrators). Sort by fee volume to see what pricing levels active earners are running.

### 3. Is my setup stable enough?

Missing reward rounds is permanent lost LPT. Receiving jobs you can't complete damages your performance reputation with gateways. Both require reliable node operation — not production-grade necessarily, but reliably online and monitored.

Home setups with unreliable internet connections, power cuts, or machines that get turned off are real viability risks. If you can't maintain ~95%+ uptime, the pool worker path is more appropriate.

The pool worker path is **significantly lower barrier** than solo orchestrating. If you're new to Livepeer and have a GPU but limited LPT, start there. You can always migrate to solo orchestrating later after building understanding of the network.

## AI vs transcoding: which to start with?

If you have a high-VRAM GPU (24 GB+), AI inference is worth evaluating as a starting point — or in addition to transcoding.

<StyledTable variant="bordered">
  <TableRow header>
    <TableCell header></TableCell>
    <TableCell header>Video transcoding</TableCell>
    <TableCell header>AI inference</TableCell>
  </TableRow>
  <TableRow>
    <TableCell>**Entry requirement**</TableCell>
    <TableCell>Active set stake (high)</TableCell>
    <TableCell>Capability registration (low)</TableCell>
  </TableRow>
  <TableRow>
    <TableCell>**Revenue stability**</TableCell>
    <TableCell>Stable, continuous for active streams</TableCell>
    <TableCell>Variable, demand-dependent</TableCell>
  </TableRow>
  <TableRow>
    <TableCell>**GPU dependency**</TableCell>
    <TableCell>Recommended, not required</TableCell>
    <TableCell>Required</TableCell>
  </TableRow>
  <TableRow>
    <TableCell>**Competition level**</TableCell>
    <TableCell>Highly competitive (mature network)</TableCell>
    <TableCell>Growing but less saturated (as of 2026)</TableCell>
  </TableRow>
  <TableRow>
    <TableCell>**Revenue per job**</TableCell>
    <TableCell>Low per segment</TableCell>
    <TableCell>Higher per inference job</TableCell>
  </TableRow>
  <TableRow>
    <TableCell>**Setup complexity**</TableCell>
    <TableCell>Lower</TableCell>
    <TableCell>Higher (aiModels.json, model downloads)</TableCell>
  </TableRow>
</StyledTable>

AI inference is not a guaranteed shortcut to revenue — demand for specific pipelines varies, and running cold models earns very little. But the reduced stake barrier makes it accessible to new operators with capable hardware.

## Useful tools before you commit

Check these before setting up a node:

- **[explorer.livepeer.org/orchestrators](https://explorer.livepeer.org/orchestrators)** — See current active orchestrators, their stake, fee volumes, and commission rates. Understand what you'd be competing against.
- **[tools.livepeer.cloud/ai/network-capabilities](https://tools.livepeer.cloud/ai/network-capabilities)** — Check which AI pipelines and models are currently being served by the network, and at what prices.
- **[Livepeer Discord — #orchestrators](https://discord.gg/livepeer)** — Active orchestrators discuss current earnings, pricing strategy, and hardware. The best source for current market conditions.
