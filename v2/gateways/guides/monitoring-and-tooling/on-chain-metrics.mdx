<Note>
  This page applies to **video gateways** (on-chain) and **dual gateways** only. Off-chain AI gateways do not interact with Arbitrum contracts — no on-chain debugging applies to them.
</Note>

Prometheus metrics show what your gateway process reports about itself. On-chain debugging shows what is actually happening at the contract layer on Arbitrum — where ETH is moving, which tickets orchestrators are redeeming, and whether gas costs are affecting your payment system. These two views complement each other; neither replaces the other.

---

## How Livepeer Payments Work On-Chain

Your gateway issues probabilistic payment (PM) tickets to orchestrators with each transcoded segment. Most tickets are worth zero — but a fraction are "winning" tickets that the orchestrator can redeem on-chain for the full face value. This lottery design keeps most payments off-chain while the Arbitrum `TicketBroker` contract handles winning ticket settlement.

Your deposit and reserve on `TicketBroker` fund all ticket redemptions. When your deposit is exhausted:
- Orchestrators begin rejecting your tickets
- Job routing silently stops
- Prometheus shows it via `livepeer_gateway_deposit → 0`, but you need Arbiscan to see *why* it depleted (which orchestrators redeemed, how much, when)

## Arbiscan — Your On-Chain Window

[Arbiscan](https://arbiscan.io) is the block explorer for Arbitrum One. For gateway operators, it is the primary tool for:

- Viewing all transactions from your gateway address
- Watching TicketBroker redemption events (who redeemed, how much)
- Monitoring deposit and reserve changes
- Checking gas price history

### Find Your Gateway on Arbiscan

Your gateway's ETH address is shown in `livepeer_cli → Get node status` under **ETH Account**. Navigate to:

```
https://arbiscan.io/address/<YOUR_GATEWAY_ETH_ADDRESS>
```

Key tabs to use:

| Tab | What you find |
|-----|--------------|
| **Transactions** | All outgoing transactions from your address (bridging, deposits) |
| **Internal Txns** | Contract-initiated transfers — ticket redemptions will appear here |
| **Token Transfers** | ERC-20 token movements (LPT bridging) |

### TicketBroker Contract Events

The `TicketBroker` contract emits events for every deposit, reserve, and redemption action. These are the events to watch:

{/* REVIEW: Mehrdad (Protocol) — confirm exact event names on current TicketBroker contract on Arbitrum One */}

| Event | What it means | When it fires |
|-------|--------------|---------------|
| `FundDeposit` | You added ETH to your gateway deposit | When you run `livepeer_cli → Deposit broadcasting funds` |
| `FundReserve` | You added ETH to your reserve | When you set reserve during deposit flow |
| `RedeemWinningTicket` | An orchestrator redeemed a winning ticket against your deposit | Each winning ticket redemption by an orchestrator |
| `Unlock` | Withdrawal unlock period started | When you call unlock on deposited funds |
| `Withdraw` | Funds withdrawn after unlock period | When you withdraw after the unlock period |

To find redemptions against your gateway: filter Arbiscan events by the TicketBroker contract address, then filter by `RedeemWinningTicket` events where the `sender` field matches your gateway address.

### Set Up Arbiscan Watchlist Notifications

Arbiscan allows you to receive email alerts when your address is involved in a transaction. This is the fastest way to detect unexpected deposit activity.

1. Create an Arbiscan account at [arbiscan.io](https://arbiscan.io)
2. Navigate to **My Account → Watch List → Add Address**
3. Enter your gateway ETH address
4. Select notification triggers: "Transaction sent to" and "Transaction sent from"
5. Enable email notifications

You will receive an email each time a ticket redemption occurs against your deposit, allowing you to catch unexpected drain rates before your deposit is exhausted.

## Verify Deposit State On-Chain

Prometheus shows your deposit via `livepeer_gateway_deposit`, but you can also query the TicketBroker contract directly to verify the node-reported value against chain state.

### Via livepeer_cli

```bash
livepeer_cli -host 127.0.0.1 -http 5935
# Select: "Get node status"
# Look for: BROADCASTER STATS → Deposit, Reserve
```

### Via Arbitrum RPC (eth_call)

You can query the TicketBroker contract directly via JSON-RPC to get deposit and reserve independently of the running node:

{/* REVIEW: Mehrdad — confirm getSenderInfo function signature and encoding for TicketBroker on Arbitrum */}

```bash
# Encode call to getSenderInfo(<your_gateway_address>)
# Function selector: first 4 bytes of keccak256("getSenderInfo(address)")
# This is an advanced technique — see Arbitrum/Solidity docs for ABI encoding

curl -X POST https://arb1.arbitrum.io/rpc \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{
    "jsonrpc": "2.0",
    "method": "eth_call",
    "params": [{
      "to": "<TICKET_BROKER_CONTRACT_ADDRESS>",
      "data": "<ENCODED_CALL>"
    }, "latest"],
    "id": 1
  }'
```

For most operators, `livepeer_cli` is sufficient. Direct contract queries are useful when the node is offline or when verifying that node-reported state matches chain state.

## Gas Price Monitoring

Arbitrum One gas prices fluctuate. When gas is expensive, orchestrators pay more to redeem winning tickets — this cost is ultimately reflected in their pricing to gateways. High gas periods can cause orchestrators to increase prices above your `-maxPricePerUnit` cap, leading to routing failures even when your deposit is healthy.

### Check Current Gas Price

```bash
curl -X POST https://arb1.arbitrum.io/rpc \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{"jsonrpc":"2.0","method":"eth_gasPrice","params":[],"id":1}'
```

The response is in wei. Convert to Gwei (divide by 1e9) for a human-readable figure.

### Prometheus Gas Metric

```promql
# Current suggested gas price from node's oracle (in wei)
livepeer_suggested_gas_price

# Convert to Gwei
livepeer_suggested_gas_price / 1e9
```

Alert when `livepeer_suggested_gas_price` spikes significantly above its baseline — this is a leading indicator that orchestrators may raise prices above your max, causing job routing to slow.

### Response to Gas Spikes

During sustained high-gas periods:

1. **Raise `-maxPricePerUnit` temporarily** to keep jobs routing to orchestrators absorbing higher redemption costs
2. **Monitor `livepeer_payment_create_errors`** — increases here during gas spikes indicate ticket-related failures
3. **Use USD-mode pricing** (`-priceFeedAddr`) if you prefer stable USD-denominated caps that auto-adjust with ETH price → see [Pricing Strategy](/v2/gateways/guides/payments-and-pricing/pricing-strategy)

## Track Deposit Depletion Rate

Understanding how fast your deposit depletes helps you plan top-ups before interruptions occur.

### From Prometheus

```promql
# Deposit depletion rate (wei per second, negative = spending)
deriv(livepeer_gateway_deposit[1h])

# Estimated time to deposit exhaustion at current rate (seconds)
livepeer_gateway_deposit /
  abs(deriv(livepeer_gateway_deposit[1h]))
```

### From Arbiscan

Sort `RedeemWinningTicket` events by timestamp to see redemption frequency and value patterns. Unusually large or frequent redemptions from a single orchestrator may indicate that orchestrator is experiencing high gas costs, or in a worst case, receiving a disproportionate share of winning tickets.

## Diagnosing "Gateway Stopped Routing" Without Obvious Error

If your gateway stops routing jobs without clear error messages, work through this checklist in order:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check Prometheus deposit gauge">
    `livepeer_gateway_deposit` — if zero, top up immediately. If non-zero, continue.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check livepeer_cli">
    `Get node status → BROADCASTER STATS` — both Deposit and Reserve must be non-zero.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check Arbiscan for recent redemptions">
    Look for a burst of `RedeemWinningTicket` events in the last 30–60 minutes. A sudden burst may have drained your deposit faster than expected.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test Arbitrum RPC">
    `curl -X POST <ethUrl> -d '{"jsonrpc":"2.0","method":"eth_blockNumber","params":[],"id":1}'` — if this fails, your RPC is the problem, not your deposit.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check gas price">
    `livepeer_suggested_gas_price` — if gas spiked, orchestrators may have raised prices above your `-maxPricePerUnit`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify orchestrator availability">
    Visit [Livepeer Explorer](https://explorer.livepeer.org) to confirm your configured orchestrators are active.
  </Step>
</Steps>
